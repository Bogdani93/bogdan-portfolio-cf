About OneSite!

Problem

OneSite was born out of a real need identified by our Sales team and CEO. They noticed that event managers were struggling to create online, in-person, and hybrid events quickly and easily. Traditional event planning tools were too complicated and time-consuming, which often led to events that were poorly executed and failed to meet the expectations of attendees.

Solution

OneSite was designed to solve these problems by providing a simple and intuitive platform that allows events managers to create events in a fraction of the time it would take with traditional tools. As a UX/UI designer I worked to create a user-friendly interface that is accessible to everyone, regardless of their technical skills. With OneSite, event managers can focus on what really matters: creating unforgettable experiences for attendees. The first version of the platform will focus on the launch of Onesite and it will be aimed at obtaining leads and inviting our possible clients to try out the product.

UX Design Process

Below are the design stages I followed for this project. These were determined by the timeframe, project scope and resources available.

01. Discover 02. Research 03. Vision & Validation 04. Visual Design 05. Final Product

Understanding what the clients need

To develop OneSite, we first had to understand the needs of our clients. We worked closely with our Sales team to gather feedback from clients who were struggling with event planning. This feedback was essential in developing a platform that would meet the needs of our clients. We listened carefully to their pain points and worked to develop solutions that would address them. I used a variety of techniques to understand the clients' needs, including user interviews and surveys. This allowed us to gain valuable insights into what our clients were looking for in an event planning tool. By leveraging our Sales team's interactions with clients, we were able to create a platform that is not only easy to use but also addresses the specific needs of event managers.

User research

After gathering feedback from our Sales team and clients, the next step was to analyze the data and transform it into actionable insights. I conducted an in-depth analysis of the feedback to identify common pain points, needs, and expectations. From there, I developed user personas to represent the different types of event managers who would be using OneSite. These personas were based on the needs and characteristics of our clients, and allowed me to better understand their goals and priorities. By developing these personas, I was able to prioritise features and functionalities that would be most useful to our clients. Our user-centered design approach ensured that OneSite was tailored to the needs of event managers and provided them with a seamless experience when planning their events. Ultimately, the personas helped me to better understand our clients, their needs, and how OneSite could help them achieve their goals.

User personas

Through our analysis of the feedback, I identified three major user groups for OneSite. These groups were further divided into two groups based on their spending habits, some valuing the price the most while others were not really bothered by the budget.

  • The first group comprises event managers who may struggle with technology due to their limited technical skills.
  • The second group consists of tech-savvy event managers who are comfortable with technology and expect project-related costs only.
  • The third group is tech-savy and values simplicity and wants a platform that can be set up quickly and easily in as few steps as possible with his team.

Low fidelity wireframes

As I began creating the wireframes, I was able to collaborate with the product team to iterate and refine the designs based on their feedback. By starting with low-fidelity wireframes, I was able to identify potential design issues early on in the process, saving time and resources in the long run. This process allowed me to create a solid foundation for the final product design that met the needs of both the stakeholders and the end-users.

User testing

Furthermore, I conducted 6 remote moderated tests by giving participants access to the Figma prototype of the wireframes. The findings were then split into three priority levels: Major, Moderate and Minor.

  • Major: Users found the personalisation of their OneSite a bit confusing
  • Moderate: The sign up for new form was not clear in regards to what it is for
  • Major: Users struggled with understanding the purpose and functionality of certain buttons and icons.

High-fidelity wireframes

High-fidelity wireframes were a critical component in the design process for OneSite. These wireframes served as a detailed visual representation of the product's user interface, providing a clear understanding of the product's layout, functionality, and user experience. These high-fidelity wireframes were crucial in conveying the design vision to stakeholders and developers and ultimately contributed to the success of the Onesite product.
